Resources

Here is a list of the resources we found helpful, and that you might too. Any websites we sited, we took information from, or we looked to for direction are listed here, and are available to the public.

HIV Wikipedia

Wikipedia is a good source when it comes to multiple aspects of HIV and AIDS. This includes everything from virology and classification, to genetic viriability, to origins. Wikipedia is also the gateway to more than 160 linked resources which are cited on the webpage.

Visit the HIV Wikipedia page: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HIV

Centers for Disease Control and Prevetion

Official government website. Many articles about HIV. Very Informational and resourceful. Incudes HIV basics, HIV risk and prevention, Statistics, amongs many other helpful links.

Visit the CDC website: https://www.cdc.gov/hiv/default.html

HIV Government Page

Government official website. Includes information about HIV and Aids. Talks about how it's transmitted and who is at risk, among many other things

Visit the HIV.org website: https://www.hiv.gov/hiv-basics/overview/about-hiv-and-aids/what-are-hiv-and-aids

Planned Parenthood HIV and AIDS

Planned Parenthood focuses strongly on sex ed and being a physicial resource. They have clinics and provide all sorts of support. Their website talks about PrEP, a huge step for HIV and AIDS. They also include more information about the disease

Visit the Planned Parenthood website: https://www.plannedparenthood.org/learn/stds-hiv-safer-sex/hiv-aids

Mayo Clinic HIV and AIDS

Has an overview of HIV. Not only includes how HIV and AIDS spread, but how it doesn't. Talks about risk factors, which could potentially apply to you or a loved one. Includes section about infections common to HIV/AIDS.

Visit the Planned Parenthood website: https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/hiv-aids/symptoms-causes/syc-20373524

Healthline's guide to HIV and AIDS

Very easy to navigate. Many clickable categories, from HIV tests to HIV symptoms in men and women, to HIV life expectancy to many other things.

Visit the Healthline website: https://www.healthline.com/health/hiv-aids
